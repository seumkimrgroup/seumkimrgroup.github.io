Lab Introduction
We explore liquid crystal materials, electro-optical devices, and simulation-driven photonic engineering.
Our work spans from liquid crystal composite materials and electro-optical modulation to computational optimization for thermal, electrical, and optical systems.
Rather than being defined by a single narrow topic, the lab connects materials, devices, and modeling into a broader photonic research workflow.
Research Topics
01
LC composite materials and systems
Synthesis, formulation, and application of liquid crystal composite materials for responsive optical and functional systems.
02
LC-based electro-optical systems
Development of phase modulators, tunable optical elements, and anisotropy-driven electro-optic components from visible to near-infrared.
03
ML-assisted optimization of FEM simulations
Data-driven thermal, electrical, and optical simulation workflows for faster device analysis and scalable design exploration.
